Triple Nickles

True story of bravery and resilience of the 555th all-Black Parachute Infantry Battalion.

During WWII, the Japanese began attaching incendiary bombs to hydrogen balloons and sending them across the Pacific Ocean in an attempt to wreak havoc on the infrastructure of the United States. During operation “Fu-Go” almost ten thousand of these bombs were launched, igniting hundreds of forest fires in the Pacific North West.
In an attempt to deceive the Japanese intelligence services, the United States opted to keep the cause of these fires classified, not publicly acknowledging the flames and suppressing media coverage. The goal being to convince the Japanese that their tactics were ineffective. The “Triple Nickels” were quietly deployed to fight the fires and become some of the first “Smoke Jumpers”. This top secret deployment was known as Operation Firefly. The 555th PIB were chosen for this mission, stems from segregation era policies and prejudices. As the only “colored” paratroopers, these men had been prevented from deploying to Eroupe or the Pacific Theater. Their war was here at home. Their war was amongst the flames.
